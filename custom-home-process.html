Custom Home Process

Research

Building a new home is not an easy task. Taking the time to familiarize yourself with the entire process and asking some initial questions makes the construction of a new home a memorable and gratifying experience.
  • Are you sure you are ready to build?
  • What is your budget?
  • How much house do you need?
  • Where do you want to build?
  • How long do you plan to be there?
  • Do you need any special accommodations?
  • What is your time frame?
Realistic expectations will make building your new home part of the “American dream” and not a nightmare.

Pre-construction

Now that you have decided to build determining a design that fits your needs and identifying all the materials and finishes must be specified. This is the time when some of the most important decisions are made. Robert Nolte Construction is able to make alterations to your designs to help fit your needs. We can also help with choosing materials and finishes. It is during this time we will determine the budget and timeline for your project.

Site Work

This is the first physical work on the house. Clearing trees, building roads, spreading rock, and digging for the foundation. You can now start to see where your house will sit and how big it will be.

Foundation

The foundation of your house needs to be strong, square, and dry. This is not a good place to save money. Robert Nolte Construction uses proven materials and methods to insure you will have a solid foundation.

Framing

Framing for many is the most exciting part. The structure takes shape very fast and you can finally walk around your new home. Robert Nolte Construction specializes in framing and takes great pride in constructing the home you have been dreaming about. We will work closely with the homeowner to make sure all the details are right.

Rough-ins

Robert Nolte Construction works closely with a team of talented tradesmen that will now be converging on your new home. This is a time when you will be making many decisions on the inner workings of your house. Plumbing, HVAC, lighting, electrical, phone lines, security systems, fireplaces, television, computer networks, central vacuum lines, will all be getting installed. Getting things right at this stage is key to a smooth finish.

Interior Finishes

Once rough-ins are completed its time to insulate, finish the walls, install doors and trim, hang cabinets, install hardwood floors, ceramic tile, paint, and stain. After painting, light fixtures, counter tops, plumbing fixtures, mirrors, shower doors, door knobs, security systems, towel bars, and paper holders can all be installed.

Exterior Finishes

While work is progressing inside there will be much going on outside as well. Robert Nolte Construction can help you make an informed decision on your choice of exterior siding. Whether you choose, brick, stone, stucco, vinyl, concrete, or wood, we will make sure your house is protected from the elements, with minimal maintenance. Be sure to leave an adequate budget for landscaping. Walks, patios, decks, and plantings can be a sizeable expense but is important in blending the structure with nature.

Completion

Rest assured that the team of quality craftsmen that built your home will be available to assist you with any issues that come about on your new home long after it is complete. We strive to make everyone a satisfied customer.
